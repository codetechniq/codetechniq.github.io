Hackathon Theme: Eradicating boarders

We now live a world of communication. Thanks to technology; learning about cultures and countries on the other side of the world can be achieved by the click of a button. But along with this ease and access came a downside: Technology also allows fake news, negative feelings and ideas to become easy to spread, and with them, fear of "the other". In the recent years, this has brought about a great deal of social issues, more severe immigration constraints around the world, countries refusing refugees and asylum seekers and the spreading of steryotypes.
The borders some of us face on a daily basis are not necessarily physical ones keeping us out of countries. They can be borders of racism or steryotypes that deprive us of opportunities, and borders that prevent us from expressing our individuality and showing the world who we are.
Nowadays, it is easier than ever to communicate with people miles away, but true connections are harder to make. This leads to rising levels of loneliness. A study by Kaiser Family Foundation in partnership with the Economist found that technology was contributing to the rise of feelings of loneliness all over the world, with over 9% of adults in Japan, 22% in America and 23% in Britain reporting that they always or often feel lonely, lack companionship, or else feel left out or isolated.

The goal of this hackathon is to encourage our participants to think of a solution that can bring people together and tackle your personal view of "borders". Be creative and work on an app, website or any platform of your choices, that focuses on human connection and communication; focus on what unites us as people and not what divides us.
Eradicating boarders can be about: Destroying steryotypes platforms for different people to meet Foreigner/Japanese people meetup Volunteering with the differntly-abled Spreading good thoughts Bringing people from different cultures together Teaching about other cultures/religions/countries Strengthening connections

“No one is born hating another person because of the color of his skin, or his background, or his religion. People must learn to hate, and if they can learn to hate, they can be taught to love, for love comes more naturally to the human heart than its opposite.”
Nelson Mandela
